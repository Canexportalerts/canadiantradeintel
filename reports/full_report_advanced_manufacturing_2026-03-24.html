# Trade Intelligence Brief

Regulatory stability dominated this week across major sanctions and export control frameworks, with no changes recorded to OFAC's Specially Designated Nationals List (18,714 entries), the UN Security Council Consolidated List (1,002 entries), or the US BIS Entity List. This absence of new designations suggests a holding pattern in enforcement activity, though Canadian exporters should not interpret stasis as reduced compliance vigilance—screening protocols remain non-negotiable for any US-bound shipments or transactions involving listed entities.

The Canada Gazette publishing cycle failed to yield actionable regulatory intelligence this week due to metadata-only summaries in available feeds. This gap underscores a critical dependency: Canadian manufacturers relying on official gazette notices for export control updates should establish direct monitoring of specific instrument types rather than depending on automated RSS aggregation, particularly ahead of any potential Q1 regulatory announcements affecting advanced manufacturing sectors.

Canadian exporters with supply chain exposure to US-origin content or technology should verify their entity screening infrastructure now, before regulatory changes accelerate post-inauguration cycles. The current quiet period is an operational window to audit customer lists, refresh OFAC checks, and confirm that sanctions-screening software reflects the current baseline of 18,714+ designations.
