Canada runs a significant and growing merchandise surplus with the United Kingdom, driven dramatically in 2024 by a surge in gold exports. The UK joined CPTPP in 2024, adding a new multilateral dimension to a relationship already governed by the Canada–UK Trade Continuity Agreement (CUKTCA). Historic Commonwealth ties, shared legal frameworks, and common language make the UK the most naturally accessible major market for Canadian businesses outside North America.

The 2024 trade figures reflect two distinct dynamics: the gold export surge (precious metals dominate Canadian export figures to the UK in peak years) and underlying strength in aerospace components, machinery, and premium agri-food. The UK market for Canadian products is genuinely deep — Canada is among the top five non-EU export sources for the UK across multiple categories.

Key Canadian exports: Gold and precious metals, aircraft and aerospace components, machinery, pharmaceutical products, agri-food (canola, pulses, seafood), lumber.

Key Canadian imports from UK: Motor vehicles (Jaguar Land Rover), machinery, spirits (Scotch whisky), pharmaceutical products, chemicals.

02

Market Opportunity

The UK's post-Brexit reconfiguration creates genuine new opportunities for Canadian businesses willing to engage actively. In areas where the UK previously relied on EU supply chains or EU-headquartered companies, there is now a structural opening for Canadian alternatives — particularly in professional services, financial services, technology, and critical minerals.

Financial & Professional Services
London remains the world's leading financial centre for certain products — FX trading, insurance (Lloyd's), asset management. Canadian financial institutions (all Big Five banks have London presences) have deep UK market access. The permanent Canada–UK FTA under negotiation could unlock significantly deeper services access than CUKTCA currently provides.
Critical Minerals
The UK has its own critical minerals strategy targeting supply chain diversification away from China. Canadian lithium, cobalt, and nickel producers are explicitly identified as preferred supplier candidates. The UK's battery manufacturing ambitions (Northumberland gigafactory) create direct demand for Canadian mineral inputs that CUKTCA tariff-free access supports.
Aerospace & Defence
Canada–UK aerospace ties are among the deepest of any bilateral relationship. Bombardier (Belfast operations), Pratt & Whitney Canada (Rolls-Royce partnership), and BAE Systems Canada are all active. UK defence spending is increasing toward 2.5% of GDP — creating significant procurement opportunities for Canadian defence companies with established UK relationships.
Technology & Digital
CUKTCA's digital trade provisions (and CPTPP's more modern framework) prohibit data localization between Canada and the UK, creating a frictionless environment for Canadian tech companies serving UK clients. The UK's fintech and AI sectors have natural Canadian partnership markets given regulatory alignment and language compatibility.
Agri-food & Premium Products
99% of Canadian goods enter the UK duty-free under CUKTCA. Canadian premium products — seafood, maple products, organic grains, craft spirits — have identifiable UK consumer demand. Post-Brexit, the UK is actively rebuilding its agri-food import infrastructure and Canadian suppliers have preferential access that many competitors lack.
Government Procurement
CUKTCA preserves the CETA-level procurement access that gave Canadian companies the right to bid on UK government contracts above defined thresholds. UK public sector spending on technology, infrastructure, and professional services is substantial. Canadian companies should be actively monitoring UK government procurement portals.
03

Investment Climate

The UK offers one of the world's most stable and sophisticated investment environments — deep capital markets, strong rule of law, independent judiciary, and globally respected financial regulation. For Canadian investors, the UK has always functioned as the primary European base of operations, and this remains true post-Brexit despite the additional complexity of UK-EU regulatory divergence.

Post-Brexit complications are real but manageable. Companies that previously used a UK entity to passport services across the EU now require separate EU establishments. This has led some Canadian financial institutions to expand their Dublin or Amsterdam presences alongside London. But for Canadian businesses primarily interested in the UK domestic market — 68 million consumers, $3.1T GDP — Brexit has changed the regulatory landscape without diminishing the underlying market opportunity.

Major Canadian investors in the UK: All Big Five Canadian banks (London is the primary non-North American hub for RBC, TD, Scotiabank, BMO, CIBC), Brookfield Asset Management (major UK infrastructure portfolio), Bombardier (Belfast aerospace), Manulife (UK wealth management), CPPIB and Ontario Teachers (London offices for European investment management), and Lululemon (European HQ).

05

Trade Agreement Coverage

CUKTCA⚑ Bridge Agreement — FTA in Negotiation
Canada–UK Trade Continuity Agreement. In force April 1, 2021. Preserves CETA-level access post-Brexit — 99% of Canadian goods enter the UK duty-free. Government procurement access preserved. A bridge agreement while the permanent Canada–UK Free Trade Agreement is negotiated. This is the most significant active FTA negotiation Canada currently has open.
Rules of origin caution: EU-origin inputs no longer count toward Canadian content under CUKTCA. If your product contains European components, your rules of origin calculation under CETA does not automatically apply under CUKTCA. Verify with your customs broker.

07

Procurement Pipeline

CUKTCA's government procurement chapter gives Canadian companies the legal right to bid on UK central government contracts and contracts from certain public bodies above defined financial thresholds. UK public sector procurement is substantial — the government spends approximately £300B annually across departments, agencies, and public bodies.

Where to find opportunities: Find a Tender Service (FTS) is the UK's official procurement portal — all above-threshold central government contracts must be published here. Defence procurement is handled separately through the Ministry of Defence procurement pages.

Active procurement sectors (2024–2026): Defence and security (UK government has committed to 2.5% NATO spending target — significant new procurement pipeline), digital and technology (HMRC, NHS, and Home Office all running major digital transformation programmes), infrastructure (Great British Railways, ULEZ expansion, offshore wind), and professional services.

⚑ WATCH
The UK's Integrated Review Refresh (2023) increased defence spending commitments significantly. Canadian defence and aerospace companies — particularly those with established UK relationships — should be actively tracking MoD procurement notices.
