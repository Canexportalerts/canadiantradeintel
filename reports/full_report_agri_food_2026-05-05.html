🔗 Supply Chain Watch

Import dependencies, critical input signals, and inbound supply chain risks for Canadian businesses in this sector this week.

Domestic grain handling & logistics capacity
Rail and port disruptions are costing Canada's grain sector up to $540 million/week, while AAFC projects 2026-27 wheat production at 29.3 MT (down 11%) and canola at 19.2 MT. Simultaneously, Saudi Arabia's large wheat purchase (985,000 MT for June-August delivery via Red Sea ports) signals strong international demand that Canadian suppliers cannot fully capture due to domestic infrastructure constraints.
→ Map alternative logistics routes (inland container terminals, cross-border US rail partnerships, barge capacity on Great Lakes/rivers) and quantify incremental costs vs. revenue loss from port/rail delays. Prioritize contracts with flexible delivery windows or pre-position inventory at regional distribution hubs to bypass peak congestion periods.
High
Food input cost inflation and retail demand volatility
Canadian grocery prices rose 4.4% YoY (March 2026), outpacing national inflation at 2.4%. Food bank usage hit 2.2M monthly visits (double 2019 levels) with 52% of food banks reducing distribution due to supply constraints. Consumer behavior is shifting sharply toward discount retailers, signaling sustained demand pressure and input cost pass-through risk.
→ Conduct price elasticity analysis for your key commodity inputs and finished goods; negotiate multi-year contracts with suppliers at fixed or capped rates where possible. Audit sourcing of lower-cost inputs (bulk grains, proteins, packaging) from alternative domestic or CEPA-eligible suppliers to protect margin under sustained inflation.
High
India market entry and tariff exposure under CEPA
Canada-India CEPA schedules are now live with certification and rules-of-origin requirements in effect. This represents both a sourcing opportunity (lower-cost inputs from India under preference tariffs) and a competitive threat (Indian ag-food exports to Canada may increase). Tariff data and compliance rules are actively being updated.
→ Register for ISED's mid-week CEPA webinar and contact Global Affairs Canada Trade Commissioner Service (India Desk) to assess duty impacts on your inbound inputs and competitive exposure. Pre-qualify for EDC trade finance to evaluate pilot sourcing agreements with Indian suppliers if input cost reduction is a priority.

Government Primary Sources

  • Statistics Canada — Canadian International Merchandise Trade (CIMT) (Monthly — current through ~2 months prior) — Detailed monthly import and export data at the 6-digit HS commodity level. Selectable by country, province, year and month. Exportable to CSV. Best source for current bilateral goods trade flows by sector.
  • Statistics Canada — The Daily: International Merchandise Trade (Monthly — published with ~5 week lag) — Monthly press release covering Canada's merchandise trade with top 10 partners, NAPCS sector breakdown, and balance of payments data. Released approximately 5–6 weeks after reference month.
  • Statistics Canada — Canada and the World Statistics Hub (Varies by indicator; generally annual with some quarterly series) — Interactive bilateral data hub covering trade, investment, employment and travel for 18 of Canada's major trading partners. Brings together multiple StatCan datasets in a single country-level analytical tool.
  • ISED Canadian Importers Database (2023 (CBSA-sourced) — NOTE: typically lags ~2 years; 2024 not yet released as of April 2026) — Company-level list of major Canadian importers by product (HS code), city, and country of origin. 'Major importers' = companies collectively accounting for up to 80% of imports of a given product. Includes customs brokers and non-resident importers.
  • ISED Trade Data Online (Monthly — current through recent months) — Monthly aggregate bilateral trade flows for Canadian imports and exports, by HS product and NAICS industry, for 200+ countries. Sourced from Statistics Canada and U.S. Census Bureau.
  • Global Affairs Canada — Canada's State of Trade (2024 full-year (2025 report)) — Annual comprehensive report on Canada's international trade and economic performance. Covers merchandise exports/imports, services trade, FDI flows in and out, sector-by-sector performance, and the economic outlook. 2025 edition covers full-year 2024 data.
  • Global Affairs Canada — Canada's Response to U.S. Tariffs (Current — actively updated) — Live policy page covering Canada's response to U.S. tariffs including relief measures, support programs, retaliatory measures, and sector impacts. Frequently updated — key cross-sector risk signal.

Audit sourcing of lower-cost inputs (bulk grains, proteins, packaging) from alternative domestic or CEPA-eligible suppliers to protect margin under sustained inflation.

India market entry and tariff exposure under CEPA
Canada-India CEPA schedules are now live with certification and rules-of-origin requirements in effect. This represents both a sourcing opportunity (lower-cost inputs from India under preference tariffs) and a competitive threat (Indian ag-food exports to Canada may increase). Tariff data and compliance rules are actively being updated.
→ Register for ISED's mid-week CEPA webinar and contact Global Affairs Canada Trade Commissioner Service (India Desk) to assess duty impacts on your inbound inputs and competitive exposure. Pre-qualify for EDC trade finance to evaluate pilot sourcing agreements with Indian suppliers if input cost reduction is a priority.
