CGC commits $325 million to Alberta wallboard plant and Nova Scotia quarry

Share LinkedIn X

Western Canada is gaining a major wallboard manufacturing hub as CGC Inc. commits $325 million across two projects: a new Wheatland facility in Alberta and revitalization of the Little Narrows gypsum quarry in Nova Scotia. The company named Sean Maynard as Plant Manager for Western Canada operations to oversee the expansion.

The Wheatland wallboard plant represents the first major drywall manufacturing investment in Alberta in over a decade. Located to serve the Calgary-Edmonton corridor, the facility will supply building materials for the province's residential and commercial construction boom driven by interprovincial migration.

"We're seeing sustained demand from population growth and infrastructure development," said a CGC executive familiar with the project timeline. The plant will employ approximately 150 workers when operational in 2027.

The investment couples Alberta manufacturing with Nova Scotia resource extraction through the Little Narrows gypsum quarry revival. Maritime gypsum will supply raw materials for Western wallboard production, creating an integrated coast-to-coast supply chain.

For Alberta's construction industry, local wallboard production reduces transportation costs and supply chain vulnerabilities that emerged during recent building material shortages. The facility positions the province as a manufacturing center rather than solely a resource extraction economy.
