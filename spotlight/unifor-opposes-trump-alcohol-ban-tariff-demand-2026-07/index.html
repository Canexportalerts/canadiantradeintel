Washington Demands Canada Scrap Alcohol Ban or Face Deeper Trade Pain

Share LinkedIn X

Unifor, Canada's largest private-sector union, issued a formal statement this week opposing U.S. President Donald Trump's latest tariff proclamations, which include an explicit demand that Canada reverse its ban on American alcohol imports. Canada imposed that ban as a countermeasure after earlier U.S. trade aggression. Unifor's intervention signals that organized labour — not just corporate lobbies — is now a frontline actor in the trade war.

In plain language: the U.S. is telling Canada to undo a defensive trade measure Canada enacted to protect its own workers. If Ottawa complies, it weakens its negotiating hand on every other file. If it refuses, American retaliation expands.

For Canadians working in distilleries, breweries, and grocery retail — particularly in Ontario and British Columbia where provincial liquor policy intersects with federal trade law — this dispute could affect shelf prices and supplier contracts within weeks.

Watch for: whether the federal government publicly backs Unifor's position or quietly signals willingness to negotiate the alcohol ban away as part of a broader deal.
