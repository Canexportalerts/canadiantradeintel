· Canada Domestic Immediate
Canada Growth Fund invests C$145 million in North American Lithium mine expansion
On May 12, 2026, Minister Champagne announced Canada Growth Fund investment of up to C$145 million to support expansion of Canada's largest operating lithium mine in Quebec's Abitibi region. The brownfield expansion will increase production capacity and strengthen North American lithium supply chain, supporting 252 existing jobs. Additionally, on May 11, NRCan highlighted over $100 million for five BC critical minerals projects.
What This Could Mean For YouProvides major growth opportunities for Canadian lithium exporters and battery supply chain companies, while strengthening domestic critical minerals processing capacity. The Quebec investment directly supports Canadian mining operations competing globally, while BC infrastructure investments position Canadian miners to access new export markets.

· India Canada This Quarter
India-Canada commence second round of CEPA trade talks targeting 2026 completion
India and Canada kicked off second round of CEPA negotiations on May 5, 2026, following March 2 signing of Terms of Reference during PM Carney's India visit. The comprehensive agreement targets USD 50 billion bilateral trade by 2030 and includes critical minerals cooperation, uranium supply arrangements, and clean energy partnerships. Bilateral trade currently stands at $8.66 billion (FY25).
What This Could Mean For YouMajor opportunity for Canadian critical minerals exporters to access India's rapidly growing market through preferential trade terms. CEPA would provide structured framework for Canadian uranium, lithium, and potash exports while attracting Indian investment in Canadian mining projects. Success critical as Canada competes with other suppliers for India's $770 billion critical minerals demand by 2040.

Cameco signed $2.6B uranium supply deal with India on March 2, 2026
Cameco secured a nine-year agreement to supply 22 million pounds of uranium concentrate to India's Department of Atomic Energy from 2027-2035. The deal was signed during Canadian PM Mark Carney's visit to India and witnessed by PM Modi and Saskatchewan Premier Scott Moe.
What This Could Mean For YouMajor boost for Canada's uranium sector, particularly Saskatchewan which employs over 2,300 in uranium mining contributing $2.5B to provincial GDP. Strengthens Canada's position as global uranium supplier and enhances bilateral trade with India's expanding nuclear program targeting 100 GW by 2047.

· Nickel Mining Canada This Quarter
Vale Base Metals announces 195,000 metres of drilling planned for 2026 in Sudbury basin to extend mine life
Vale Base Metals unveiled plans for an aggressive 195,000 metres of drilling in 2026 to boost mineral reserves and resources in the Sudbury basin, with exploration in 2025 delivering significant results and increasing total mineral resources to 1.7 million tonnes of copper and 1.4 million tonnes of nickel. The company expressed confidence in increasing total mineral reserves and resources in Canada and Brazil by more than 20% by late 2027.
What This Could Mean For YouThis represents a major commitment to expanding Canada's nickel and copper resource base in the world's largest single nickel district. Successful results could significantly extend mine life and production capacity for Canadian nickel exports, supporting domestic supply chain security for EV battery materials.
