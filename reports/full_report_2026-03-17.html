⚡ Intelligence Signals This Week
· Canada Tech Export Immediate
Canada launches new CanExport SMEs 2026-27 with $31 million funding emphasizing defence sector prioritization and export
The Canadian government launched the CanExport SMEs 2026-27 program with approximately $31 million in total funding, with only $3.1 million allocated to U.S. markets to encourage export diversification. The program now requires minimum 3 full-time employees and $300,000 annual revenue (increased from 1 FTE and $100,000), and explicitly prioritizes defence sector projects as part of the Defence Industrial Strategy. Per diem rates increased to $600 from $400.
What This Means For YouThis represents a strategic shift toward non-U.S. export markets and defence sector emphasis, directly supporting Canadian tech companies' international expansion efforts. The increased eligibility thresholds target more established SMEs with greater export capacity.
Source: Trade Commissioner Service Canada
· Canada Cyber Export Immediate
Canada joins EU's €150 billion SAFE defence program, becoming the only non-European nation with preferential access to m
Prime Minister Carney confirmed Canada has concluded negotiations to join the EU's Security Action for Europe (SAFE) program worth €150 billion ($244 billion CAD), with formal launch expected in coming weeks. The deal integrates Canada into European maritime and cyber defence supply chains, including underwater surveillance sensors and AI-enabled electronic warfare systems. This aligns Canada with Europe's Readiness 2030 supply chain and joint procurement groups.
What This Means For YouThis marks a strategic realignment toward Europe for Canadian defence and cybersecurity exporters, providing preferential access to massive European defence procurement while reducing reliance on U.S.-only partnerships. It opens new market access channels for Canadian cyber and maritime security companies.
Source: NATO Association of Canada
