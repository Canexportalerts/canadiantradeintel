Japan is Canada's most important bilateral relationship in the Indo-Pacific and the country most transformed by CPTPP. Before CPTPP entered into force in December 2018, Canada and Japan had no bilateral trade agreement — Japanese tariffs on Canadian agri-food products, energy goods, and industrial products were a persistent barrier. CPTPP eliminated or began phasing out those tariffs, creating immediate export gains for Canadian canola, pork, beef, seafood, and lumber exporters.

Canadian exports to Japan declined in 2024 — driven primarily by drops in shipments to Hong Kong, China, and Japan combined. The Japan-specific decline reflects yen weakness (making Canadian imports more expensive in local currency terms) and softening Japanese industrial demand rather than any structural loss of market access. The CPTPP tariff advantage remains and is growing as phase-out schedules continue.

Key Canadian exports: Canola, coal, lumber, pork, seafood, aircraft, machinery, potash, soybeans.

Key Canadian imports from Japan: Motor vehicles (Toyota, Honda, Mazda, Subaru), auto parts, machinery, electronics, steel.

02

Market Opportunity

Japan's decarbonization agenda — one of the most ambitious in the G7 — creates sustained demand for Canadian inputs across energy, critical minerals, and cleantech. Japan cannot produce the clean energy resources it needs domestically. Canada can supply them.

LNG & Clean Energy
Japan is the world's largest LNG importer and a primary target market for LNG Canada (the Kitimat terminal). Japanese utilities have signed long-term offtake agreements with Canadian LNG projects. Japan's hydrogen import strategy explicitly targets Canadian green and blue hydrogen as a preferred supply source.
Critical Minerals
Japanese automotive and electronics manufacturers have urgent demand for Canadian lithium, cobalt, nickel, and graphite for EV battery supply chains. JOGMEC (Japan's state minerals agency) has active Canadian critical minerals investment programs. Several Japanese companies have taken equity stakes in Canadian mining projects.
Agri-food & Seafood
CPTPP tariff phase-outs continue to improve access for Canadian canola, pork, beef, and seafood. Japan's premium food market values Canadian product quality and food safety standards. Canadian seafood — particularly Pacific salmon, crab, and scallops — commands premium prices in Japanese retail markets.
Technology & Digital
Japan's technology sector is undergoing significant digital transformation — aging population, labour shortages, and industrial automation needs create demand for Canadian AI, robotics, and enterprise software. CPTPP's digital trade provisions protect cross-border data flows between Canada and Japan.
03

Investment Climate

Japan offers a stable, rule-of-law investment environment with strong intellectual property protection. The primary complexity for Canadian investors is cultural and linguistic rather than legal or regulatory — Japanese business culture emphasizes relationship-building over transactional speed, and the market typically rewards patience and long-term commitment.

Japanese investment into Canada has been growing significantly — JOGMEC critical minerals investments, Toyota's battery supply chain investments in Canadian lithium, and pension fund infrastructure exposure via CPPIB and Ontario Teachers' Tokyo offices. The bilateral investment relationship is increasingly reciprocal in ways it was not a decade ago.

Major Canadian investors in Japan: Manulife (through John Hancock Japan and Manulife Insurance Japan), CPPIB and Ontario Teachers (Tokyo investment offices managing Asian allocations), and LNG Canada (targeting Japanese utility offtake partners).

05

Trade Agreement Coverage

CPTPPIn Force — Dec 2018
Before CPTPP, Canada and Japan had no trade agreement. CPTPP was transformative — eliminating or phasing out tariffs on 95%+ of goods, including significant agri-food tariffs that had long disadvantaged Canadian exporters. Japan is CPTPP's largest economy and the most significant new market access Canada gained from the agreement.
Key provisions for Canadian businesses: Agri-food tariff phase-outs (canola, pork, beef, seafood all benefit); digital trade provisions protecting cross-border data flows; investment chapter strengthening Canadian investor protections; government procurement access for Japanese public contracts above thresholds.

⚑ WATCH
China's application to join CPTPP — if approved — would fundamentally alter the agreement's character and Canada's strategic calculus within it. Current members have not reached consensus on China's bid. Japanese opposition to China's membership is significant and influential. Canadian Government Signals

📄
Indo-Pacific Strategy · Global Affairs Canada · 2022
Canada's Indo-Pacific Strategy identifies Japan as a "like-minded" anchor partner and a priority destination for Canadian trade and investment diversification. Japan is explicitly named as a critical minerals supply chain partner in the strategy's resources section.
📄
Canada–Japan Critical Minerals Partnership · NRCan · Ongoing
Natural Resources Canada has active bilateral critical minerals cooperation with Japan's Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI) and JOGMEC. Joint investment frameworks support Japanese equity participation in Canadian mining projects with strategic minerals relevance for Japan's industrial supply chains.
