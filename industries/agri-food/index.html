Canada's agri-food position
Trade context
Canada feeds the world — and depends on access to do it
Canada is the world's fifth-largest agricultural exporter, generating over $90 billion in annual agri-food exports. Prairie farmers produce 25–30% of global traded durum wheat, 20% of global canola, and supply 65% of India's lentil imports. Any tariff, quota, or market access decision in Canada's top buyer countries — India, China, the US, Japan — directly affects farm income on 40,000+ prairie farms.

Key market signals
Canola
China SAMR restrictions shape $3.2B annual exposure
Canada sends over 40% of canola seed exports to China. SAMR inspection restrictions are the primary political lever Beijing uses in bilateral disputes. Canada-EU CETA provides a partial diversification route — canola oil exports to EU grew 35% since CETA implementation.
Pulses
India DGFT quota cycles drive Saskatchewan and Manitoba income
Canada controls 65% of India's lentil import market. India's DGFT issues annual pulse import quotas that directly set volumes and trigger price moves within 48 hours of announcement. Saskatchewan lentil farmers earn $1.2–1.8B annually — most of it India-dependent.
Wheat
Black Sea disruption sustains Canadian Hard Red Spring premium
Russia-Ukraine conflict has permanently shifted global wheat trade flows. Canadian Hard Red Spring wheat commands a quality premium in MENA, Southeast Asian, and East African markets that Black Sea supply cannot fully meet. CP and CN rail capacity to Vancouver is the choke point.
Seafood
Atlantic lobster: $2.5B export industry with US and China exposure
Atlantic Canada's lobster industry is the largest single seafood export product — $2.5B annually. US market access and China tariff dynamics both apply. PEI potato access to the US remains a recurring tension point. Nova Scotia and NB processors are the value-add frontier.
