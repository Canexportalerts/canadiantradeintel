· Canada Agri Trade Immediate
Canada secured major tariff breakthrough with China effective March 1, 2026, reducing canola seed tariffs from 84% to ap
On January 16, 2026, Prime Minister Mark Carney reached a preliminary agreement with China to lower canola seed tariffs to 15% by March 1, 2026. China also removed 100% tariffs on Canadian canola meal, peas, lobsters and crabs from March 1 through December 31, 2026. The deal affects approximately $4 billion in annual canola seed exports and $2.6 billion in other agricultural products.
What This Means For YouThis breakthrough restores competitiveness for Canadian canola farmers after prolonged trade barriers and provides market certainty for the 2026 seeding season. The deal is expected to increase canola demand from 7 million tonnes to potentially 8-8.5 million tonnes.
Source: Seed World
· China Canola Immediate
China concluded anti-dumping investigation on February 28, 2026, imposing final 5.9% duties on Canadian canola seed for
China's Ministry of Commerce announced final anti-dumping duties of 5.9% on Canadian canola seed imports effective for five years, added to the existing 9% MFN duty for total duties of 14.9%. This concludes the investigation that began in September 2024. Combined with the January 2026 trade agreement reducing overall tariffs to approximately 15%, this represents significant improvement from previous 84% combined rates.
What This Means For YouThe final determination provides certainty for the $3.2 billion annual Canadian canola export market to China, replacing the temporary 75.8% preliminary duties. The lower final rate, combined with the trade agreement, restores Canadian competitiveness in China's crucial oilseed market.
Source: Canola Council of Canada

· India Pulses This Quarter
India imposed 30% tariff on yellow pea imports effective November 1, 2025, and analyst expects lentil duties to increase
India implemented a 30% tariff on peas from all origins on November 1, 2025, significantly impacting Canadian pea exports. AgPulse Analytica analyst Gaurav Jain forecasts India will raise lentil import duties from current 10% to 30% starting April 1, 2026, driven by strong domestic production and government support prices.
