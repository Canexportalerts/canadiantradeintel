Critical Minerals & Cleantech — Week of March 18, 2026

# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY Six Russian-linked entities were removed from US OFAC sanctions this week, with removals concentrated in Turkey's digital printing and AI technology sectors, suggesting potential shifts in enforcement priorities rather than broader policy relaxation. This development opens potential business pathways for Canadian manufacturers in printing technology, computer equipment, and AI services to reassess previously restricted Turkish and Russian parties—though the removal pattern warrants cautious evaluation before engagement. No changes appeared on the UN Consolidated List or US BIS Entity List, though Canadian exporters must independently review Canada Gazette Part II publications (January 14–March 11, 2026) at gazette.gc.ca, as export control and sanctions amendments may not be captured in available summaries. Canadian exporters in critical minerals and cleantech should cross-reference any new Turkish or Russian contacts against updated OFAC records before proceeding with commercial discussions.

· Canada Trade Deals Immediate
Canada signed multiple critical minerals trade agreements in the past 14 days, including a $12.1 billion Critical Minera
On March 2, 2026, Canada announced 30 new partnerships worth $12.1 billion under the Critical Minerals Production Alliance with 12 allied nations. Canada signed MOUs with Saudi Arabia on January 13, 2026 for mineral resource cooperation, and joint declarations with the EU and Greenland on critical minerals collaboration. What This Means For YouThese agreements position Canada as a global critical minerals hub while securing new export markets and investment opportunities for Canadian mining companies. · Canada Domestic Immediate
Canada announced over $3.6 billion in new critical minerals programs including a $2 billion Critical Minerals Sovereign
On March 3, 2026, Minister Tim Hodgson announced $165.2 million for 22 Canadian critical minerals projects unlocking $434 million in project capital across eight provinces. The new $2 billion Critical Minerals Sovereign Fund will provide equity investments and offtake agreements starting spring 2026. The $1.5 billion First and Last Mile Fund replaces the Critical Minerals Infrastructure Fund to accelerate mine development and processing infrastructure.
What This Means For YouThese investments directly support Canadian mining companies and projects from exploration to processing, creating jobs and ensuring domestic supply chain development. The sovereign fund represents unprecedented government backing for critical minerals development in Canada.
Source: Natural Resources Canada
· Lithium Market Immediate
Volkswagen signed a $48 million investment and binding 10-year lithium offtake deal with Canadian Patriot Battery Metals
Volkswagen and PowerCo invested US$48M for 9.9% stake in Patriot Battery Metals and secured binding offtake of 100,000 tonnes of spodumene concentrate annually for 10 years from Quebec's Shaakichiuwaanaan Project. The lithium will supply PowerCo's St. Thomas gigafactory in Canada (90 GWh capacity) and European facilities, with deliveries expected to start when production begins.
What This Means For YouMajor opportunity for Canadian lithium sector with Volkswagen as first OEM to directly invest in Canadian upstream lithium mining. Validates Quebec as strategic EV supply chain location for North American and European markets.
Source: Volkswagen Group
· Uranium Market Immediate
Cameco signed a $2.6 billion CAD uranium supply agreement with India for 22 million pounds over 9 years starting 2027.
Cameco signed long-term contract to supply nearly 22 million pounds of uranium concentrate to India's Department of Atomic Energy from 2027-2035, valued at approximately CAD$2.6 billion. Agreement signed during PM Mark Carney's visit to India on March 2, 2026, with India targeting 100 GW nuclear capacity by 2047 from current 24 reactors.
What This Means For YouMassive revenue boost for Canadian uranium sector representing one of largest export contracts. Strengthens Canada's position as key nuclear fuel supplier to India's ambitious expansion program and validates Saskatchewan mining operations.
Source: Cameco Corporation
