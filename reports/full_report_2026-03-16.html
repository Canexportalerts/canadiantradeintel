⚡ Intelligence Signals This Week
· Canada Agri Trade Immediate
Canada secured major trade agreement with China on January 16, 2026, cutting canola seed tariffs from 84% to 15% by Marc
China is expected to lower combined tariffs on Canadian canola seed to approximately 15%, down from current levels near 85%, by March 1, 2026. Canadian canola meal, peas, lobsters, and crabs will be exempt from anti-discrimination tariffs beginning March 1, 2026, until at least the end of the year. China's final ruling implemented a 5.9 percent anti-dumping tariff plus 9 percent import duty on Canadian canola seed, alongside suspension of 100 percent tariffs on canola meal effective March 1.
What This Means For YouChina represents a $4-billion market for Canadian canola producers, making the tariff reduction one of the most consequential agricultural trade developments for Canada in recent years, expected to restore competitiveness for Canadian farmers and exporters. The agreement provides welcome clarity for farmers heading into the 2026 seeding season.
Source: Seed World
· China Canola Immediate
China's MOFCOM announced final anti-dumping determination on February 28, 2026, establishing 5.9% anti-dumping duty on C
China's Ministry of Commerce announced a final determination establishing a 5.9% anti-dumping duty on Canadian canola seed imports for five years, which will be added to the pre-existing 9% MFN duty, resulting in total duties of 14.9%. MOFCOM also announced an adjustment of the 100% tariff on canola meal to 0% for March 1, 2026 to December 31, 2026, allowing meal to trade into China tariff free. Canola oil continues to be subject to a tariff of 100%.
What This Means For YouThe final anti-dumping ruling provides certainty for Canadian canola exporters after months of investigation uncertainty. Canadian exports of canola and canola products to China were valued at approximately $5 billion in 2024, with 2025 export value expected to plummet to less than half that amount.
Source: Canola Council of Canada
· Wasde Wheat This Quarter
USDA February 19, 2026 outlook projects 6% decline in US wheat production to 1.86 billion bushels for 2026/27 marketing
The USDA's February 19, 2026 Grains and Oilseeds Outlook shows US wheat production projected to drop 6% to 1,860 million bushels due to reduced harvested area and lower yields. Total wheat planted area for 2026/27 is forecast at 45.0 million acres, down 0.3 million acres from last year. The outlook also shows stronger soybean production expectations with planted area forecast to rise nearly 4 million acres, reflecting stronger profitability compared to other crops.
What This Means For YouLower US wheat production creates export opportunities for Canadian wheat producers. The USDA report notes heightened Northern Hemisphere competition from Russia, EU and Canada with collective exportable supplies similar to 2025-26, positioning Canada well to capture market share.
Source: USDA World Agricultural Outlook Board

· Potash Fertilizer Immediate
Trump administration launches DOJ price-fixing investigation targeting Canadian fertilizer companies including Nutrien,
President Trump announced Monday a Department of Justice investigation into alleged price-fixing by foreign fertilizer companies and threatened tariffs on Canadian imports. Nutrien, the world's largest potash producer, is a key target. The US depends on Canada for over 80% of its potash demand. Current potash prices are around US$350 per tonne, well below the US$500+ incentive price needed for new mine development.
Risk AssessmentMajor threat to Canadian potash exports as US market represents over 50% of Canadian potash exports. Could force diversification to other markets like Brazil and China. However, analysts suggest price-fixing accusations are misplaced as potash market is well-supplied globally.

India imposed 30% tariff on pea imports from all origins effective November 1, 2025, and analysts expect lentil import d
India announced a 30% tariff on peas of all origins effective November 1st, while lentil tariffs currently sit at 10%. Analyst Gaurav Jain expects the Government of India to hike the existing 10% lentil duty to 30% as of April 1, 2026.
