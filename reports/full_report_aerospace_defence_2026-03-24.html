# Trade Intelligence Briefing

Regulatory compliance lists remained static this week across US OFAC, UN Security Council, and Canadian designations, with no new sanctions designations affecting aerospace and defence supply chains. The stability offers a narrow window for companies to execute pending transactions, though vigilance remains essential given geopolitical volatility in Eastern Europe and the Indo-Pacific.

Canada Gazette publications this week contained only scheduling notices rather than substantive regulatory amendments, leaving a gap in actionable compliance intelligence. However, companies should anticipate imminent rule-making from Global Affairs Canada on export controls and from Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada on manufacturing standards—both sectors critical to defence procurement alignment with NATO allies.

Canadian exporters should immediately review full Canada Gazette Part II editions directly to identify any pending orders affecting controlled goods classifications or end-use documentation requirements. The absence of changes this week does not signal reduced scrutiny; rather, it suggests regulatory shifts may come with shorter implementation timelines. Procurement teams should front-load compliance assessments now rather than risk delays during the next amendment cycle.
