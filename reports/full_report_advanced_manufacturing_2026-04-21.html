Intelligence Overview

President Trump's April 6 expansion of Section 232 tariffs to full product value - eliminating Canada's previous exemptions - immediately raises steel and aluminum input costs for Canadian manufacturers by up to 50%, forcing urgent supply chain recalibration and pricing adjustments. Advanced manufacturers dependent on US-sourced steel and aluminum must accelerate nearshoring strategies to Mexico or Mexico-Canada partnerships, or lock in long-term domestic sourcing contracts now. Watch the Spring 2026 Defence Investment Agency legislation enabling $180 billion in Canadian procurement opportunities; defence contractors can offset tariff impacts by capturing protected domestic contracts before competition intensifies.
