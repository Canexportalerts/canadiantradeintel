⚡ Intelligence Signals This Week
· Canada Domestic Immediate
NRCan announces $3.6 billion in new programs including $2 billion Critical Minerals Sovereign Fund and $1.5 billion Firs
On March 3, 2026, Minister Hodgson announced over $3.6 billion in new investments at PDAC 2026, including $165.2 million for 22 Canadian projects unlocking $434 million in project capital. The new Critical Minerals Sovereign Fund will provide $2 billion over five years through equity investments, debt instruments, and offtake contracts. The First and Last Mile Fund replaces the Critical Minerals Infrastructure Fund with $1.5 billion until 2030.
What This Means For YouThese funds directly support domestic mining development and processing capacity across eight provinces. The sovereign fund represents Canada's first dedicated critical minerals investment vehicle to accelerate mine-to-market development and reduce foreign dependency.
Source: Government of Canada
· Uranium Conversion Enrichment Immediate
DOE awarded $2.7 billion in uranium enrichment contracts on January 6-7, 2026, marking the largest domestic capacity exp
U.S. DOE announced $2.7 billion in uranium enrichment task orders on January 6-7, 2026, awarding $900 million each to Centrus Energy, General Matter, and Orano Federal Services for LEU and HALEU production over the next decade. Orano targets NRC license submission in early 2026 for its Oak Ridge facility with 2031 production start. General Matter plans construction beginning 2026 at Paducah site with operations by 2030.
What This Means For YouCameco's Port Hope conversion facility remains one of only four Western UF6 conversion plants, creating strategic leverage as U.S. enrichment capacity expands. The massive DOE investment validates long-term uranium demand fundamentals and could drive higher realized prices for Canadian uranium producers.
Source: POWER Magazine

On January 25, 2026, western Canadian provinces and territories signed an MOU for a joint Western Canadian Critical Minerals Strategy. Canada also signed MOUs with Saudi Arabia (January 13), EU, and Greenland for critical minerals cooperation.

· India Canada This Quarter
India and Canada launch CEPA negotiations and sign $2.6 billion uranium deal with critical minerals MOU during PM Carney
On March 2, 2026, PM Modi and PM Carney signed Terms of Reference for CEPA negotiations targeting completion by end-2026, aiming to boost bilateral trade to $50 billion by 2030. Cameco secured a $2.6 billion uranium supply deal (22 million pounds, 2027-2035). Both countries signed an MOU on Critical Minerals Collaboration covering lithium, cobalt, and rare earth elements to strengthen supply chain resilience.
What This Means For YouThis represents a major export opportunity for Canadian uranium and critical minerals producers. The CEPA could provide preferential market access to India's 1.4 billion population market, while the uranium deal creates long-term revenue certainty for Saskatchewan-based Cameco.
Source: Prime Minister of Canada
· Rare Earth Processing This Quarter
DOE announced $2.7 billion in uranium enrichment contracts on January 6-7, 2026, with over $900 million for rare earth s
U.S. DOE awarded $2.7 billion in contracts to Centrus, General Matter, and Orano for domestic uranium enrichment capacity expansion on January 6, 2026. Ucore reported dysprosium oxide prices reaching $1,000/kg outside China versus $200/kg inside China, demonstrating severe pricing gaps. Multiple rare earth refining projects are advancing with 2026 timelines including Ucore's Strategic Metals Complex in Louisiana targeting H2 2026 commissioning.
What This Means For YouCanadian rare earth producers like Vital Metals with its Nechalacho mine face critical midstream bottlenecks, as separation capacity outside China remains extremely limited. The price premiums (5x for dysprosium outside China) create significant opportunity if Canadian producers can secure non-Chinese processing partnerships.
Source: War on the Rocks
· Graphite Processing This Quarter
Global Battery Materials launched with Canada-Korea alliance to build ex-China graphite anode supply chain by 2028.
Global Battery Materials formed through strategic merger of Korean processor SongWoo EM and Canadian G6 Energy, owning Ontario's Kearney Mine. The company plans to restart Canada's only previously producing natural graphite mine and expects active anode material production by 2028. Northern Graphite also announced Saudi Arabia's first battery anode plant in Yanbu, processing Namibian graphite for 2.5 million EVs annually.
What This Means For YouCanadian graphite miners now have processing partnerships outside China, creating complete mine-to-battery supply chains. This breaks the China processing bottleneck for Canadian projects like Nouveau Monde and Mason Graphite.
Source: Charged EVs
