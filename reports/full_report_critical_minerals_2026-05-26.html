What Happened This Week

BMI, the research unit of Fitch Group, reported this week that global shipments of Chinese export-controlled rare earths - including neodymium, dysprosium, and terbium used in permanent magnets for EV motors and defence equipment - have remained well below historical levels despite a summit between US President Trump and Chinese President Xi. No diplomatic breakthrough loosened China's export controls, which have been active since April 2025.

Intelligence Overview

Mangrove Lithium opened North America's first commercial electrochemical lithium refinery in Delta, BC on April 16, 2026, producing battery-grade lithium hydroxide at scale for the first time domestically. This facility eliminates Canadian dependence on foreign refining for EV battery supply chains and positions BC-based manufacturers as competitive suppliers to North American automakers. What We Think
— CTI Editorial Desk  ·  May 26, 2026

This week marks a watershed moment for Canadian critical minerals exports with India and Indonesia emerging as transformed opportunities through new trade agreements and financing commitments. The $2.6 billion uranium deal with India and Export Development Canada's $825 million Indonesia commitment signal a strategic pivot toward Asian markets that could define the sector for years. While established markets like the United States and Europe remain stable with strong fundamentals, the real action is in emerging partnerships where Canadian producers can establish first-mover advantages before competitors catch up. The G7 Critical Minerals Alliance's $18.5 billion mobilization provides the financing framework for Canadian companies to capitalize on these opportunities, but speed matters – windows for preferred supplier status close quickly once established players enter new markets.

6. Supply Chain Watch
Rare earth elements (yttrium, dysprosium, terbium)
Chinese export controls have cut US yttrium imports by 95% (333t to 17t) and driven prices 69× higher since November 2025. Canadian importers face identical supply risk and price exposure through North American supply chains.
ACTIONAudit current yttrium and rare earth element inventory levels and contract terms immediately. Activate India-US rare earth corridor sourcing pathway via Trade Commissioner Service (tcs.india@international.gc.ca) to explore alternative supply before pricing locks further.
High
Sulfur (mining processing input)
Global sulfur supply shortages are now constraining mining processing capacity. Canadian processors dependent on sulfuric acid and elemental sulfur imports face input cost escalation and availability tightening.
ACTIONContact Statistics Canada's commodity-input price tracking (WPI for sulfur derivatives) and confirm supplier inventory positions with existing sulfur/sulfuric acid vendors. Identify domestic or regional alternative processors if sulfur-dependent operations are at risk.

African and Latin American mining asset access / critical mineral feedstock
Chinese miners (Zijin Mining and peers) are rapidly consolidating mining assets across Africa and Latin America (May 27–28, 2026). Simultaneously, resource nationalism in Ecuador, Brazil, and Mexico is imposing new royalty regimes (3–8%) and compliance barriers. Canadian businesses face both asset competition and regulatory cost escalation for inbound ore/concentrate sourcing.
ACTIONReview existing Latin American and African supply contracts for force majeure and royalty-adjustment clauses.
