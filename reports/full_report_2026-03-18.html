⚡ Intelligence Signals This Week
· Canada Trade Deals Immediate
Canada secured 30 new critical minerals partnerships worth $12.1 billion with 12 allied nations on March 2, 2026.
Canada announced partnerships with the EU, Greenland, India, and 9 other countries through the Critical Minerals Production Alliance. The total capital supported by the Alliance now reaches $18.5 billion. Western provinces also signed a new MoU on January 25, 2026 to develop a unified critical minerals strategy expected by June 2026.
What This Means For YouThese deals strengthen Canada's position as a trusted supplier and diversify export markets beyond traditional partners, creating opportunities for Canadian mining companies to access new investment and offtake agreements.
Source: Government of Canada
· Canada Domestic Immediate
Canada launched $2 billion Critical Minerals Sovereign Fund and $1.5 billion First and Last Mile Fund at PDAC 2026 on Ma
NRCan Minister Tim Hodgson announced $3.6 billion in new programs including $165.2 million for 22 Canadian projects unlocking $434 million in project capital. The Sovereign Fund will provide equity investments, debt instruments and offtake contracts. The First and Last Mile Fund replaces the Critical Minerals Infrastructure Fund to support mine development and processing infrastructure.
What This Means For YouThese funds directly support Canadian mining companies and infrastructure development, providing government-backed financing to accelerate project development and reduce private sector investment risks in critical minerals value chains.
Source: Natural Resources Canada
· Uranium Conversion Enrichment Immediate
DOE awards $2.7B in uranium enrichment contracts with first production-scale task orders to expand domestic LEU and HALE
US Department of Energy issued $900M each to Centrus Energy, General Matter, and Orano Federal Services on January 7, 2026 for domestic uranium enrichment expansion. Orano targets initial LEU production in 2031 at Oak Ridge facility, while General Matter plans 2030 startup at Paducah using existing UF6 inventory avoiding $800M disposal costs.
What This Means For YouMajor boost for Western uranium conversion/enrichment capacity reduces bottlenecks for Canadian uranium producers like Cameco. New US facilities provide alternative pathways to realize higher uranium prices and reduce dependence on Russian enrichment services, directly benefiting Canadian mine-to-market strategies.
Source: Power Magazine
· India Canada This Quarter
India and Canada signed critical minerals MoU and launched CEPA negotiations on March 2, 2026, targeting completion by e
Prime Ministers Modi and Carney signed Terms of Reference for Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement negotiations aiming to increase bilateral trade to $50 billion by 2030. A $2.6 billion uranium deal between India and Cameco was announced, supplying 22 million pounds from 2027-2035. Critical minerals cooperation covers lithium, cobalt and rare earth elements supply chains.
What This Means For YouCEPA could significantly expand Canadian critical minerals exports to India's growing market, while the uranium deal creates long-term revenue certainty for Cameco and strengthens Canada's position in nuclear fuel supply chains.
Source: Prime Minister of Canada
· Graphite Processing This Quarter
Canadian company Global Battery Materials announces new leadership appointments to scale ex-China graphite and anode pro
On March 17, 2026, Global Battery Materials Corp. (GBM) appointed Jamie Horvat as CFO and Joon Bae Lee as CTO to advance commercial-scale production. The company combines a prior-producing Canadian graphite mine with a patented anode processing pilot plant in South Korea, targeting qualification with battery cell manufacturers in North America and Asia.
What This Means For YouThis directly creates new offtake opportunities for Canadian raw graphite producers like Nouveau Monde and Mason Graphite by providing a processing pathway outside China's 95% anode capacity dominance.
Source: Cantech Letter
· Lithium Refining Capacity This Quarter
Ex-China lithium hydroxide production faces commissioning delays and weaker demand dynamics affecting Western refining c
Wesfarmers' Covalent Lithium refinery at Kwinana experiencing extended production ramp-up due to intermittent odor issues. Market participants cite operational constraints at Albemarle's Kemerton plant and broader challenges for Western lithium hydroxide assets amid weaker high-nickel cathode demand following US EV subsidy cuts in September 2025.
Risk AssessmentCanadian spodumene producers face reduced access to non-Chinese conversion capacity as Western refineries struggle with technical issues and commissioning delays. This constrains Canadian lithium miners' ability to access premium Western markets and long-term offtake agreements.
Source: Fastmarkets
