Editorial Policy

Our commitment

CTI publishes trade and business intelligence for Canadian businesses. Our editorial commitment is to accuracy, source transparency, timeliness, and usefulness. Every claim we publish is sourced from a verifiable primary source and checked before publication.

Sources

CTI draws primarily from official Canadian government sources — Statistics Canada, Global Affairs Canada, NRCan, ISED, CBSA, EDC, BDC — supplemented by international government and institutional sources including the World Bank, IFC, OECD, WTO, and allied-nation trade ministries. We do not rely on press releases as sole sources for factual claims. Commercial or industry sources are used for context and are identified as such.

A full list of sources used in our pipeline is maintained at canadiantradeintel.ca/methodology.

Fact-checking

Every CTI intelligence brief is processed through an automated fact-checking system that cross-validates specific factual claims against primary government sources before publication. Claims that cannot be verified are either corrected, removed, or flagged with a [VERIFY] notation visible in the full report. Our fact-checker has a standing claims database of key statistics that is updated when primary sources change.

Independence

CTI does not accept payment to influence the substance of intelligence reporting. Sponsored content, if offered in future, will be clearly identified as such and will not affect the editorial independence of our sector reports. We have no financial relationships with companies mentioned in our intelligence content that could create editorial conflicts. Our procurement intelligence covers opportunities from public government sources — we do not accept payment to feature specific tenders.

Corrections

When we identify an error in published content, we correct it promptly and note the correction in the affected report. Significant corrections are communicated to subscribers by email. We do not silently edit published reports without notation.

AI-assisted generation

CTI's weekly intelligence reports are generated with the assistance of large language models (Claude by Anthropic) operating over primary source data collected by our automated pipeline. AI assistance does not replace editorial judgment — all content is reviewed against primary sources through our fact-checking process before publication. We disclose our methodology openly at canadiantradeintel.ca/methodology.

Limitations

Trade intelligence is inherently time-sensitive. Conditions described in CTI reports reflect the state of available information at the time of publication each Tuesday. We make reasonable efforts to ensure accuracy but cannot guarantee that all information remains current at the time of reading. Subscribers are encouraged to verify significant commercial decisions against primary sources.
