6. Supply Chain Watch
Cobalt (DRC sourced)
DRC-China March 2026 cooperation deal formalizes compliance infrastructure and monitoring committees for cobalt operations, signaling tighter Chinese control over supply chain visibility and potential export prioritization to Chinese buyers. This reduces transparency for non-Chinese importers.
→ Canadian battery and EV component manufacturers should immediately map cobalt supplier relationships in DRC and assess exposure to Chinese-backed operations. Explore alternative sourcing from Australia, Canada's own nickel-cobalt projects, or negotiated direct agreements with DRC operators before Chinese whitelist systems fully activate.
High
Lithium (global battery-grade supply)
EnergyX's Texas DLE facility now producing 250 metric tons annually of battery-grade lithium carbonate (March 2026), while China suspends export controls until November 2026. U.S.-Australia critical minerals framework commits $2.2B to new capacity by 2026. North American supply diversification is accelerating but remains tight.
→ Canadian lithium processors and battery OEMs should assess EDC co-financing eligibility for offtake agreements with EnergyX or similar North American DLE producers. Secure medium-term contracts before China's export suspension ends and Chinese allocation shifts to domestic and whitelist partners.

Critical minerals refining capacity (cobalt, lithium, nickel processing)
Global resource nationalism (40+ countries rewriting policies since 2020) and Chinese financial dominance (67% of $57B in mining loans to projects with no host government ownership) are concentrating processing control. BYD has secured six African lithium mines through 2032, signaling vertical integration away from spot markets.
→ Canadian miners and processors should engage ISED's critical minerals procurement RFPs this week to identify co-investment or offtake certainty programs. Prioritize refining partnerships with allied jurisdictions (Australia, U.S.) and secure long-term supply agreements before Chinese entities lock in African and Asian refining capacity.
