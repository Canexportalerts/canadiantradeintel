→ Canadian fertilizer manufacturers and downstream agri-input users should immediately diversify potash sourcing (Belarus, Russia alternatives) and lock in long-term contracts before tariff implementation. Monitor DOJ probe outcomes weekly via DOJ press releases and engage trade counsel on mitigation strategies.

Nitrogen-based fertilizers (urea)
Global urea prices surged 129% from $350/tonne (late 2025) to $800/tonne (March 2026). World Bank April 2026 index shows 26.2% monthly spike. Domestic Canadian supply pressure mounting as Nutrien faces investigation.
→ Grain and pulse producers should execute forward purchase agreements now for 2026–2027 seasons at current elevated rates to avoid further escalation. Contact EDC for export credit lines to finance bulk fertilizer procurement before Q3 planting.

Lentil export market access (India demand)
India's 30% tariff on peas (Nov 2025) and strong rabi pulse harvest (11.4M tonnes projected) will suppress 2026 lentil import demand. This reduces outlet pricing leverage for Canadian pulse exporters and risks input oversupply domestically.
→ Contact GAC Trade Commissioner Service (New Delhi) to confirm Q2 lentil quota eligibility and assess tariff exemption pathways. Parallel action: apply for ISED Agri-Innovation Program grant to fund competitive analysis on alternative markets (Egypt, Indonesia) for pulse exports.
