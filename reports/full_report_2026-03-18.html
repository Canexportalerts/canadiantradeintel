⚡ Intelligence Signals This Week
· Canada Domestic Immediate
NRCan announced over $3.6 billion in new critical minerals programs including $2 billion Critical Minerals Sovereign Fun
On March 3, 2026 at PDAC, Minister Tim Hodgson announced $165.2 million for 22 Canadian critical minerals projects across eight provinces, unlocking $434 million in total project capital. He launched the $1.5 billion First and Last Mile Fund (replacing CMIF) for mining infrastructure and announced the upcoming $2 billion Critical Minerals Sovereign Fund offering equity investments and offtake agreements. The fund will provide strategic investments in critical mineral projects and companies through various financial instruments.
What This Means For YouThese investments accelerate domestic critical minerals development from mine to market, supporting infrastructure, processing capacity, and Indigenous participation. The Sovereign Fund represents a new financing mechanism to get Canadian mining and processing projects operational faster while strengthening supply chain security.
Source: Natural Resources Canada
· Canada Trade Deals This Quarter
Western Canadian provinces and territories signed MoU on January 25, 2026 to develop unified critical minerals strategy,
On January 25, 2026, British Columbia, Alberta, Saskatchewan, Manitoba, Yukon, Northwest Territories and Nunavut signed an MoU to jointly advance a Western Canadian Critical Minerals Strategy, with final strategy expected by June 2026. On March 2, 2026, Canada announced 30 new critical minerals partnerships and investments worth $12.1 billion with 12 allied nations under the Critical Minerals Production Alliance. Canada also signed MOUs with Saudi Arabia (January 13) and joint declarations with EU and Greenland on critical minerals collaboration.
What This Means For YouThe Western strategy positions Canada's western regions as a unified global hub for critical minerals innovation and sustainable development, promoting Western Canada for export market access. The international partnerships unlock $18.5 billion total in critical minerals projects and strengthen Canada's position as a trusted supplier to allied nations.
Source: Government of Canada
· India Canada This Quarter
India and Canada signed critical minerals cooperation MOU and launched CEPA negotiations during PM Carney's March 2026 v
On March 2, 2026, Prime Ministers Modi and Carney signed Terms of Reference for CEPA negotiations targeting completion by end-2026, alongside MOUs on critical minerals collaboration and renewable energy cooperation. The visit included a $2.6 billion Cameco uranium supply deal (22 million pounds from 2027-2035) and agreements on lithium, cobalt, and rare earth elements supply chains. Both countries aim to increase bilateral trade to $50 billion by 2030 from current $13 billion.
What This Means For YouThe CEPA provides structured market access for Canadian critical minerals exporters to India's rapidly growing economy, while the uranium deal secures long-term revenue for Saskatchewan-based Cameco. Critical minerals cooperation reduces India's dependence on Chinese supply chains while creating export opportunities for Canadian miners.
Source: Prime Minister of Canada
