India is simultaneously one of Canada's most important future bilateral relationships and one of its most diplomatically complicated. The official goods trade figures — $15.6B in 2024 — substantially understate the full economic relationship. India is Canada's largest source of immigrants; the 1.4 million-strong Indian diaspora in Canada creates informal trade, investment, and services flows that dwarf the goods statistics. IT services, remittances, education exports, and family business networks represent billions in bilateral economic activity not captured in merchandise trade data.

Canada–India diplomatic relations deteriorated significantly following the June 2023 killing of Hardeep Singh Nijjar and subsequent mutual expulsion of diplomats. CEPA negotiations, which had been active since 2022, were suspended. The bilateral relationship is slowly recovering but the diplomatic context continues to complicate commercial engagement in ways that didn't exist before 2023.

Key Canadian exports: Pulses (lentils, peas — India is Canada's largest pulse export market), canola oil, fertilizers, precious metals, machinery, coal.

Key Canadian imports from India: Pharmaceutical products, jewelry and precious stones, machinery, organic chemicals, clothing and textiles, IT services (services not captured in goods data).

02

Market Opportunity

India's scale makes every market opportunity significant. With 1.4 billion people, a rapidly expanding middle class, and one of the world's fastest-growing major economies, India represents a generational market development opportunity for Canadian businesses willing to invest in market entry over a multi-year horizon.

Agri-food & Food Security
India is the world's largest pulse importer — Canadian lentils, peas, and chickpeas have deep market penetration. Canola oil demand is growing with India's expanding food processing sector. India's food security agenda creates ongoing demand for Canadian agri-food inputs that transcends diplomatic tensions.
Technology & Digital Services
India's IT services sector — a $250B+ industry — has deep links to Canadian tech companies. The diaspora creates natural business networks connecting Canadian enterprise software, AI, and fintech companies with Indian development and delivery partners. CPTPP's digital trade provisions don't cover India, but the commercial relationship is already substantial.
Education Services
Canada is the top destination for Indian international students — over 400,000 Indian students are studying in Canada. Education is a major Canadian export to India that doesn't appear in goods trade statistics. Canadian universities and colleges have extensive partnerships with Indian institutions.
Infrastructure & Construction
India's infrastructure investment pipeline is enormous — smart cities, transportation, energy, water. Canadian pension funds (CPPIB, Ontario Teachers, Brookfield) are significant India infrastructure investors. Canadian construction and engineering firms have project opportunities, particularly in sectors aligned with Canadian strengths.
Critical Minerals
India's manufacturing expansion — particularly in electronics and EVs — creates growing demand for critical minerals. India has prioritized supply chain diversification for lithium, cobalt, and rare earths. Canadian critical minerals producers have an opportunity to position Canada as a preferred supplier aligned with India's own China+1 strategy.
Financial Services
Canadian financial institutions have growing Indian market presence. Manulife has insurance JVs, CPPIB and Ontario Teachers have Indian equity and infrastructure investments, and the Big Five banks have correspondent relationships supporting the Indian-Canadian business community. A CEPA would significantly deepen this access.
03

Investment Climate

India has been improving its investment climate steadily — rising in the World Bank's ease of doing business metrics, simplifying FDI regulations, and building out digital infrastructure that reduces transaction costs. The GST reform, Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, and RERA (real estate regulation) have all meaningfully improved the commercial environment over the past decade.

The complexity of India's regulatory environment is real but declining. Sector-specific FDI caps remain in certain industries. The judicial system, while improving, is slow. State-level variation in regulatory application requires local expertise. Canadian businesses entering India typically do so with a local partner — the diaspora network provides natural access to trusted local relationships.

Major Canadian investors in India: CPPIB (among the largest foreign institutional investors in Indian infrastructure and real estate), Ontario Teachers (equity and infrastructure), Brookfield (one of India's largest foreign infrastructure investors), Manulife (insurance JV), Sun Life (financial services), and Scotiabank (correspondent banking).

05

Trade Agreement Coverage

CEPANegotiations Suspended
Canada–India Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement negotiations were launched in 2022 and suspended in 2023 following the diplomatic crisis. A concluded CEPA would be transformative — providing Canadian businesses with preferential access to India's 1.4-billion-person market across goods, services, investment, and government procurement.
What's at stake: Canada's agri-food exporters currently pay Indian tariffs of 30–100% on many products. A CEPA would eliminate or reduce these. Canadian IT services companies would gain improved market access. Canadian financial services firms would benefit from investment chapter provisions. The opportunity cost of no agreement is significant and growing.
Current status: Negotiations formally suspended. Diplomatic normalization underway but timeline for resumption unclear. Canadian government has signalled intent to resume when diplomatic conditions allow.
⚑ WATCH
The resumption of Canada–India CEPA negotiations is the single most significant potential trade policy development for the Canada–India relationship. Every month without an agreement is market share conceded to countries that have agreements — EU, UAE, Australia, and others have all concluded or are concluding bilateral agreements with India. CanExport Intelligence monitors Canada–India diplomatic and trade developments in our Technology and Agri-food sector reports.
06

Canadian Government Signals

📄
Indo-Pacific Strategy · Global Affairs Canada · 2022
Canada's Indo-Pacific Strategy commits $2.3B over five years to deepening Canada's engagement with the Indo-Pacific — with India identified as one of Canada's most important strategic partners in the region, particularly for critical minerals and clean energy.
📄
Canada–India Diplomatic Normalization · Global Affairs · 2024–2025
The Canadian and Indian governments have been engaged in quiet diplomatic normalization since late 2024. Both governments have signalled desire to restore the commercial relationship. TCS India programming has partially resumed. Full normalization — and CEPA resumption — remains the policy objective.
