· Wasde Wheat This Quarter
USDA released May 12 WASDE report projecting significantly lower US wheat production for 2026/27 at 1.561 billion bushel
USDA's May 12, 2026 WASDE report projects all-wheat production at 1.561 billion bushels, down 424 million from last year with yield at 47.5 bushels per acre (5.8 bushels lower than record 2025 yield). Winter wheat production is forecast down 25% to 1.048 billion bushels, primarily due to sharply reduced Hard Red Winter production. Global wheat carryout was estimated at 275.04 million tonnes versus 279.21 million for 2025-26, with world production projected at 819.06 million tonnes, down 24.78 million from previous year. Canadian wheat production estimated at 35 million tonnes with ending stocks at 4.63 million.
What This Could Mean For YouLower US wheat production and tighter global supplies create export opportunities for Canadian wheat producers. With US yields down significantly and global stocks contracting, Canadian wheat becomes more competitive in export markets, potentially boosting prices and demand for Prairie grain. Import-dependent countries may shift sourcing to Canada.
