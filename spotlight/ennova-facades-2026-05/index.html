Advanced Manufacturing Canada May 19, 2026

Windsor-based building solutions manufacturer Ennova Facades receives $4.5 million in federal funding to launch three new products and expand into new markets.

About the Company

Ennova Facades is a Windsor-based manufacturer specializing in advanced building envelope solutions and architectural facades. The company develops and produces exterior building systems that improve energy efficiency and structural performance for commercial and residential construction projects. Based in Windsor-Essex, Ontario's manufacturing heartland, Ennova represents the evolution of the region's industrial base from traditional automotive manufacturing toward advanced building technologies and sustainable construction materials.

What Happened

Ennova Facades received $4.5 million in Federal Economic Development Agency for Southern Ontario (FedDev Ontario) funding to support equipment upgrades and manufacturing modernization as part of a comprehensive facility expansion. The investment will enable the company to launch three new building solution products, expand into new markets, and enhance its manufacturing capabilities. This funding was part of a larger $20 million FedDev Ontario investment package supporting 14 manufacturers across the Windsor-Essex region.

The federal investment supports Ennova's strategy to develop next-generation building envelope technologies that meet increasing demand for energy-efficient construction solutions. The equipment upgrades will allow the company to manufacture more sophisticated facade systems with improved thermal performance and reduced environmental impact. These new products are designed to help commercial and residential builders meet increasingly stringent energy efficiency standards and green building certification requirements.

The funding also enables Ennova to expand its market reach beyond its current geographic footprint. The company plans to leverage its enhanced manufacturing capabilities to serve customers across Canada and potentially into U.S. markets, where demand for high-performance building envelope solutions continues to grow. The investment supports both domestic expansion and export development, positioning Ennova to compete with international facade manufacturers.

Why It Matters

This investment demonstrates how federal economic development programs can help regional manufacturers innovate and scale in growing sectors like sustainable construction. For Windsor-Essex, traditionally dependent on automotive manufacturing, Ennova's expansion represents economic diversification into building technologies that serve Canada's construction boom and growing emphasis on energy-efficient buildings. The region benefits from maintaining its manufacturing expertise while transitioning to new industrial sectors.

For Canada's construction sector, Ennova's product development contributes to the domestic supply of advanced building materials that help meet federal and provincial energy efficiency targets. As governments implement stricter building codes and green building requirements, Canadian manufacturers of high-performance building components become increasingly important to achieving national climate goals while supporting domestic industrial capacity.

What Others Can Learn

Manufacturers in Southern Ontario should actively engage with FedDev Ontario's manufacturing modernization programs, which provide substantial funding for equipment upgrades and facility expansions. Companies developing building technologies or sustainable materials should also explore Natural Resources Canada's Clean Technology Program and the Canada Infrastructure Bank's growth capital investments. Regional manufacturers can leverage local economic development corporations like WindsrEssex Economic Development Corporation to access federal programs and identify market expansion opportunities that build on existing industrial capabilities.
