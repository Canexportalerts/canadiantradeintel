· Canada Agri Trade Immediate
Canada secured a major breakthrough with China to slash canola tariffs from 84% to 15% by March 1, 2026, and eliminate 1
On January 16, 2026, Prime Minister Mark Carney announced a preliminary agreement with China reducing canola seed tariffs to 15% by March 1, 2026, significantly improving access to a $4 billion annual market. China also removed discriminatory tariffs on canola meal, peas, lobster, and crab from March 1 until at least December 31, 2026. What This Means For YouThis major trade breakthrough directly benefits Canadian canola farmers and processors by restoring access to their second-largest export market worth approximately $4 billion annually for seed alone. However, exporters face uncertainty as meal and pea tariff relief is only guaranteed through 2026, and canola oil tariffs remain at 100%.

· China Canola Immediate
China confirmed 5.9% final anti-dumping duty on Canadian canola seed effective February 28, 2026, substantially lower th
China's MOFCOM issued final determination on February 28, 2026, establishing 5.9% anti-dumping duty for five years, combined with 9% MFN duty for total 14.9% tariffs. This represents significant relief from previous 84% combined tariff levels and preliminary 75.8% anti-dumping duty announced in the investigation. China had cited quality concerns with Canadian canola seed related to quarantine pests and weed seeds, which CFIA disagreed with.
What This Means For YouCanadian canola exporters gain substantial relief from the final determination, as the 14.9% total tariff is dramatically lower than the 84% previously imposed. This provides greater certainty for the $3.2 billion annual canola seed export industry to China, though the five-year anti-dumping duty period creates ongoing compliance obligations and potential for future trade disputes.
