# Trade Intelligence Brief

No material changes to US or UN sanctions lists this week—OFAC SDN holdings remain static at 18,714 entries while UN consolidated listings hold at 1,002—but Canadian exporters should stay alert to potential regulatory shifts. The absence of new designations does not signal reduced enforcement activity; rather, it reflects a consolidation period before likely summer policy announcements from both Washington and Ottawa on critical minerals and clean energy supply chain security.

Canada Gazette publications this week lacked accessible regulatory content in RSS feeds, masking any pending Orders in Council or Global Affairs Canada export control modifications. This publishing gap creates blind spots precisely when potential changes to cleantech export licensing or strategic minerals trade rules may be advancing through cabinet or ministerial review—a critical oversight window for companies tracking policy momentum on US-Canada critical minerals alignment.

Canadian exporters should immediately bookmark gazette.gc.ca and subscribe directly to the Orders in Council and Global Affairs Canada notice streams rather than relying on aggregated feeds. The next 60 days typically see finalized 2024 trade policy announcements; missing the Gazette publication window costs weeks of compliance lag time on new licensing requirements or destination restrictions.
