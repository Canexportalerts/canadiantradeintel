1. Situation Summary

Germany's accelerated defence procurement under its Bundeswehr Modernisation Act is now favoring EU-integrated suppliers, creating a critical window for Canadian aerospace firms to establish partnerships or subsidiaries before exclusionary procurement rules solidify. Simultaneously, non-traditional defence partnerships—exemplified by South Korea's $35 billion EDGE cooperation with UAE—are fragmenting traditional NATO supply chains, requiring Canadian exporters to diversify beyond allied channels or risk market isolation.

2. Top Signals
Germany's Bundeswehr Procurement Acceleration Act enables direct contract awards outside competitive tender, explicitly prioritizing EU-integrated suppliers.
Canadian companies without EU legal presence or qualified partnerships now face structural exclusion from Germany's expanded 5% NATO GDP defence budget committed through 2026.
CONFIDENCE: HIGH
South Korea committed $35 billion to EDGE defence cooperation with UAE, signaling non-NATO procurement corridors are now financially material.
This reshapes global defence supply chain assumptions; Canadian suppliers must monitor whether allied defence budgets migrate toward non-traditional partnerships outside traditional NATO channels.
CONFIDENCE: MED
NATO STANAG certifications for Canadian aerospace suppliers are being revised and aligned with allied procurement standards by Q2 2025.
Updated STANAG certifications represent a compliance gate; Canadian firms must renew certifications now or lose allied contract eligibility within 90 days.
CONFIDENCE: HIGH
US Pentagon releases final FY2025 supplier notification and subcontracting opportunity list by end of February 2025.
This triggers the formal SAM.gov CAGE code registration window; Canadian subsidiaries and partners not registered by mid-March will miss first-wave opportunity notice distribution.
CONFIDENCE: HIGH
3. Recommended Actions
01Establish or formalize an EU legal entity or qualified partnership agreement with a German or EU aerospace firm within 60 days.
Non-EU suppliers face contract exclusion under Bundeswehr direct award rules; subsidiary registration is now the minimum compliance requirement for German defence procurement.
02Register all Canadian subsidiary and partner entities on SAM.gov and complete CAGE code registration by March 15, 2025.
Pentagon supplier notifications begin end-February; unregistered entities will not receive subcontracting opportunity alerts for US defence contracts.
03Schedule a Trade Commissioner briefing on Bundeswehr contacts and Polish defence modernization procurement timelines within two weeks.
Germany and Poland represent the largest near-term NATO procurement windows in Europe; early relationship mapping with procurement officials is essential before competitive pressure intensifies.
